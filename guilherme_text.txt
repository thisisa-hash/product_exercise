Since the market was regulated, Brazil has imposed a number of restrictions on the use
of bonuses and promotions in the iGaming sector. This limits the creation of more
appealing incentives and CTAs. The analysis will therefore take into account both the
screenshots provided and an overview of the local website, which is better suited to legal

primarily via a Gift Icon in the navigation bar (both desktop and mobile app), but also
through the Sportsbook Home Page and direct inner page links. This multi-entry
architecture means the measurement framework must capture both traffic source quality

The page serves two distinct player intents: discovery (players browsing available

1.3 What “success” looks like in this case
Before regulation, the Brazilian iGaming market was built around bonuses and
promotions, which proved to offer the best customer acquisition cost (CAC) in the sector.
As there were no restrictions in place, many operators offered aggressive terms to attract
new players, creating an environment where player retention was difficult (low lifetime
value, LTV) and the average deposit amount was low (GGR dependent on the value of
promotional offers).
The upside of this trend is that Brazilian players are very receptive to promotions.
Therefore, we can expect positive conversion rates from the offers page.
On the other hand, it is important to note that players coming from offers pages are of
lesser value to the operator than those from other sources. These are casual players who
will subsequently require further retention efforts to keep them playing.
Taking this information into account, along with data collected in recent years, the
product’s success in this case could be assessed as follows:
Short-Term (Weekly)
 Offer Click Rate: 10% of page sessions across all devices
 Opt-in Rate: 25% of clicks
 Exit Rate (no action): below 60%
Medium-Term (Monthly)
 Offer-to-Bet Conversion Rate: 10% (all traffic sources combined)
 Redemption-driven GGR: 12% of total platform GGR
 CRM-driven sessions converting at ≥ 1.5× the organic entry rate (CRM traffic is
higher-intent by definition)
Long-Term (Quarterly)
 Players engaging at least 2 distinct offers per month
 Offer Expiry Rate below 25% (currently untracked; baseline required)
 Bonus Cost Ratio (promotion spend / GGR attributed to offers): ≤ 25%

2.2 What I gathered from the data
The most significant finding in the dataset is the sharp deterioration in the completion
rate (opt-in conversion) during June: from a May average of 41.8% to a June average of
29.8%, a 12.0% retraction.
As it went beyond the margin, the fluctuation in the opt-in conversion rate suggests a
structural breakdown. Particularly as there was no player disengagement. The opt-in rate
(opt-in users / active users) was actually rising throughout June, from 16.7% to 17.3%.
Players continued to engage with Missions at increasing rates. They simply stopped
completing them.
So, looking at it more closely, May's peak performance (41.3% conv. rate) coincided with
maximum mission diversity. Basketball accounted for 30% of missions and Mixed
accounted for 33%. May 18, the single best week in the dataset (25,432 opt-ins; 9,765
completions), had 14 missions of which only 3 (21%) were Football, with 5 Basketball
and 6 Mixed.
June's collapse maps precisely to the elimination of Basketball (0 missions) and Mixed (0
missions), leaving Football and Tennis as the only categories. The implication is clear:
players who opt into multi-sport or Basketball missions complete them at materially
higher rates. Football-only periods suppress completion, likely because football match
schedules are less frequent and outcomes harder to control for within a 7-day mission
window.

Dopamine release builds loyalty: From another perspective, the rewards system

Customer loyalty reduces CAC: As betting operators sell commodities, one of the

Loyalty opens up new business opportunities: Once players are engaged with the

Loyalty and clustering are trends: One of the concepts most frequently discussed
in recent digital business debates is that of ‘dark forests’. Essentially, people
have been relating to the internet as if they were alone in a dark forest:
everything feels threatening, the chances of encountering a predator or bot are
high, and the opportunities for genuine connections are slim. This has been one
of the driving forces behind the rise of closed communities (Discord, Telegram,
WhatsApp) at the expense of posts on open platforms. It is also one of the
justifications for business deals such as the New York Times’ acquisition of The
Athletic, which paid over US$550 million in 2022 to gain access to a subscriber
base of 1.2 billion. Thus, the business value of such initiatives also lies in the
number of loyal users.

This introduction is necessary to make the point that, regardless of the figures involved,
loyalty programmes are extremely important for strategic reasons. This understanding
must form the starting point for any analysis of a specific scheme.

4.1 Rationale for the chosen metric
The three case studies presented in this document cover two main subjects. The first
(Offers Page) deals with user acquisition, whilst the other two (Sportsbook Missions and
Casino Loyalty) focus more on customer retention. The difference is that one of them
(Casino Loyalty) uses rewards to capture the audience’s attention, whilst the other
(Sportsbook Missions) is based on the repetition of tasks or challenges.
Therefore, my first criterion for selection was to identify the type of product in which I
have the greatest confidence. I truly believe that customer loyalty will be a major driver
of efficiency in iGaming over the coming years. This is a sector where there is little
product differentiation, and the relationship between brands and customers is essentially
transactional (offers, reactivation, bonuses, complaints, issues, rewards). The Brazilian
market has a huge volume of players, but the average GGR is low compared to countries
where the industry is more mature. Furthermore, as I pointed out above, the Brazilian
iGaming market has been built around companies that acquire users at extremely high
costs. So, any initiative that builds player loyalty and reduces CAC has a massive impact
on operational efficiency.
With this in mind, I can use my experience and knowledge of the market as a preliminary
filter for the figures.
So, looking more closely at the logic behind Sportsbook Missions, it becomes clear that
the metrics can also be grouped. Some capture only intent, such as clicks and opt-ins, but
do not necessarily indicate a business impact. Others, such as the number of missions
undertaken or the number of missions completed, depend on decisions made by parties
other than the players (if the platform decides to offer more missions, they will be able to
hit the target regardless of the conversion rate). Finally, some targets are far removed from
business impact (audience, number of active players, % of missions completed).
With this in mind, the metric I have chosen to apply within the NSM methodology is
weekly transacting mission users (WTMU), which represents the number of unique
players who entered one or more missions (opt in) and completed one or more missions
within the specified timeframe (one week). This, of course, takes into account only
transacting missions (missions with a business impact).
It is important to note that this is a complex metric, unlike those presented in the financial
reports and dashboards, and is designed to provide an in-depth insight into the operation
in question.
Furthermore, A player who opts in and completes a Mission has engaged with the full
product cycle. They experienced the incentive, placed qualifying bets, and received a
reward. This is not a passive impression.
WTMU captures the players most likely to generate incremental GGR and retain longterm. The mission completion event is the point at which the product has demonstrably
influenced player behaviour.

Step 4 — A/B Test Governance
Every product change to Missions should be validated as a randomised controlled
experiment with WTMU as the primary outcome metric. Minimum requirements: 95%
confidence threshold; minimum detectable effect of 5% relative lift; minimum 2-week
test duration (4 weeks for low-volume segments). Secondary metrics serve as guardrails,
an initiative that improves WTMU but degrades User Completion Rate or BCR beyond
thresholds should not be shipped.
